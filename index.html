Instructions: Preparation (To Saute & Puree)

  1. Pour oil to a hot pan and add green cardamoms (optional). Add the onions.
  2. Saute the onions until they are about halfway cooked (translucent, ≈ 3-4 mins). Now, add the 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste and continue saut√©ing until the onions are light golden (≈ 2-3 mins more).
  3. Add tomatoes, cashews and salt. Saute for 2 to 3 mins and cook covered until mushy and soft. If the pan dries out, add a splash of water while you cook.
  4. Cool this and transfer to a blender. Blend to a smooth puree.

Instructions: How to make Matar Paneer

  1. Heat ghee or oil in the same pan. Add cinnamon & bay leaf, let sizzle in oil for a minute.
  2. Lower the heat completely and stir in turmeric, chili powder, garam masala and coriander powder. Transfer the pureed onion tomato masala. (If your puree isn't smooth, strain it directly into the pan).
  3. Saute for 2 to 3 mins until the masala comes together and turns thick. Stir in the rinsed green peas and water to make a gravy.
  4. Bring to a boil and cook partially covered on a medium heat, until the gravy turns thick and traces of fat are visible (≈ 10-12 mins).
  5. Optionally fry paneer: Spread and heat ¬Ω tablespoon ghee in a non-stick pan and place the diced paneer. Fry on a medium high heat until golden, for 3 mins. Turn them to the other side with a tong and repeat the process.
  6. Crush kasuri methi in your palm and add it.
  7. Add slit deseeded green chili, sugar (both optional) and paneer. Mix and taste test to adjust salt and garam masala.
  8. Cook for 2 mins for the paneer to heat through and turn off. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  9. Serve matar paneer with Basmati rice, jeera rice, naan or paratha.
